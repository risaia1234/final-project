Glossary

  • Karyogram or Idiogram
    • Photograph og chromsomes in order of descending size (mostly)
    • Germ Cells
      • reproductive cells, sperm and eggs
    • Somatic Cells
      • non-reproductive cells or body cells. In other words, every other cell in the body
    • Gametes
      • A mature germ cell that can unite with the opposite sex
    • Diploid
      • having 2 sets of chromosomes in a cell
    • Haploid
      • having 1 set of chromosomes in a cell
    • Crossing Over
      • Segments of DNA exchange between homologous chromosomes
    • Sister Chromatids or Chromtids
      • Identical copies of a chromosome that are made by replicating, such as Interphase
    • Homologous Chromosomes
      • Two chromosomes that code for the same thing but not the same alleles like sister chromatids do
    • Tetrads
      • Homologous Chromosomes pairing up
    • Synapsis
      • The formation of Tetrads
    • Microtubules
      • Part of the cells skeleton, it helps in dividing cells
    • Metaphase Plate
      • An imaginary line that splits a cell in two
    • Centriole
      • The cylinder at the poles of the cells that organize the skeleton of the cell
    • Translocation
      • segment of a chromosome breaks off and reattaches itself either on another chromosome or on the chromosome it broke off of
    • Nondijunction
      • Chromosomes or Sister Chromatids don’t separate properly
    • Inversion
      • part of a chromosome detaches, flips around 180° and reattaches.
    • Centromere
      • The point where homologous chromosomes attach during cell division
    • Peiricentric
      • Detachment includes the centromere
    • Paracentric
      • Detachment doesn’t includes the centromere
